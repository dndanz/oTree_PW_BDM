You have an opportunity to play some lotteries. Your task is to report the maximum you would be willing to pay for the lottery as your offer.

Your offer will be compared to a fixed offer. The fixed offer will be randomly drawn and thus completely unrelated to your offer.

If your offer is more than or the same as the fixed offer then you receive the lottery and pay the price. Here's the interesting part. The price is not the amount you offered. Instead, the price equals to the fixed offer.

Example: if you offer $9.78 and the fixed offer is $7.23, then you buy the lottery for $7.23.

If your offer is less than the fixed offer then you do not get the lottery. Instead, you keep your money.

Example: if you offer $2.12 and the fixed offer is $5.23, then you do not get the lottery. You keep your money.

Why is it my best strategy to bid the maximum I'd be willing to pay ?

Suppose the maximum I'd be willing to pay for the lottery is $9.78 but I bid $9. Then if the fixed offer is, say, $9.1, I don't get the lottery. Had I bid $9.78, I'd receive the lottery and pay only $9.1 for the lottery that I think is worth $9.78. Suppose the maximum I'd be willing to pay for the lottery is $9.78 but I bid $10. Then if the fixed offer is, say, $9.9, I have to pay $9.9 for a lottery that I really think is worth only $9.78.
